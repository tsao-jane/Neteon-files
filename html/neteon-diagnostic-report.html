03 Three Findings That Demand Attention

The diagnostic surfaced thirteen findings.
These three are existential.

01
Identity Mismatch

AI thinks Neteon is a Moxa shop.

The desired positioning is "Edge AI partner." The AI-visible identity is "Moxa networking distributor." The Neousys and Edge AI content on neteon.ai is too thin and too recent to have shifted how LLMs describe the company.

Audit content volume ratio: networking vs. Edge AI. Target 1:1 within 90 days.
02
Claim vs. Reality

"Moxa #1 US distributor" doesn't compute.

Neteon claims top Moxa US distributor status. AI searches for that exact query surface M.A. Rugged, RS Components, and MoxaStore — not Neteon. The claim either isn't validated in third-party data, or isn't discoverable where buyers look.

Verify listing on Moxa's official distributor finder. Add schema markup: "Moxa authorized distributor."
03
Zero Authority Signal

No third-party citations exist.

Every search result is first-party: neteon.net, shopmoxa, ipc, iiot, neteon.ai, LinkedIn, ZoomInfo. No press coverage, no analyst reports, no listicle mentions, no G2 or Capterra reviews. LLMs weight third-party sources heavily — without them, Neteon cannot appear in "best X" or "recommend a Y" answers.

Pitch 2–3 industry publications (EdgeIR, RoboticsTomorrow, CNX Software) within 30 days.
